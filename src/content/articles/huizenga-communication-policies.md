Holland residents have a federal representative in Bill Huizenga—but many say they have fewer ways to reach him than ever before.

## What changed

In early 2025, constituents reported that Huizenga's Holland district office moved to **appointment-only** visits. The office also shifted phone intake toward an **automated phone tree** and web comment forms that require a valid ZIP code inside the 4th District.

[WZZM 13 reported](https://www.wzzm13.com/article/news/local/changes-in-office-policy-frustrate-constituents-bill-huizenga/69-c18ba619-83f5-4ac9-bd26-b2cf7f37375f) that protesters outside the Holland office wanted restored walk-in access and in-person town halls. Staff cited **threats against the congressman and employees**, some referred to the House Sergeant at Arms, as reasons for the policy shift.

## Virtual town halls vs. in-person demands

Huizenga's team hosted **telephone town halls** reaching tens of thousands of callers—defending them as more inclusive than a single gymnasium event. The [Holland Sentinel](https://www.hollandsentinel.com/story/news/politics/government/2025/02/28/hollanders-protest-demand-in-person-townhall-with-rep-bill-huizenga/80770694007/) documented near-daily visits by groups asking for an in-person forum; the last widely cited in-person town hall was **2018**.

## Casework vs. policy feedback

Spokesperson Brian Patrick told [MLive](https://www.mlive.com/news/kalamazoo/2025/03/flood-of-calls-prompt-gop-congressman-to-shut-off-voicemail-irking-voters.html) that routing policy calls online frees staff for **federal agency casework**—Social Security, VA, IRS—and that the office answered 30,000+ messages since January 2025. Critics counter that policy dissent is being filtered, not just organized.
